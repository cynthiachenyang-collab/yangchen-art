# Why I Want to Join Pomelo Care — Staff Data Scientist, Growth

Maternal health in the United States is in crisis, and the data makes it impossible to look away: the U.S. has one of the highest maternal mortality rates in the developed world, with stark disparities along racial and socioeconomic lines. Pomelo Care is one of the few companies attacking this problem with both clinical rigor and technological ambition. That combination is exactly where I want to spend my energy.

## The Mission Resonates Deeply

I'm drawn to work where the output isn't just a metric moving on a dashboard — it's a mom who had a healthier pregnancy, a baby who came home safely, a family that didn't fall through the cracks of a fragmented system. Pomelo's model — 24/7 virtual care, personalized care teams, value-based contracts that align incentives around outcomes — is evidence-based and scalable. That's rare. Most companies pick one or the other. Pomelo is doing both.

## This Role Is the Right Problem for Me

The Staff Data Scientist, Growth role sits at a genuinely hard intersection: translating patient behavior into enrollment and engagement strategy, building attribution models across complex multi-channel funnels, and defining the metrics that tell whether the business is actually working. These aren't toy problems. Getting enrollment right means more patients who need care actually receive it. Getting retention right means those patients stay engaged long enough to see real outcomes. I want to own that problem end to end — from exploratory analysis to roadmap ownership to cross-functional influence with marketing and product.

I've spent my career building exactly this kind of function: turning ambiguous behavioral data into clear strategic direction, collaborating closely with non-technical stakeholders, and building data infrastructure that scales with the business. A staff-level role that combines hands-on modeling with organizational leadership is the right next step for me, and Pomelo is the right place to do it.

## The Culture and Team Match How I Work Best

From everything I've read about Pomelo — from Glassdoor reviews to how the team describes itself publicly — this is a company built on genuine collaboration and mutual respect. People describe teammates as kind, willing to help, and deeply invested in the mission. The leadership team is transparent, listens actively, and doesn't pretend to have all the answers. That's the environment where I do my best work: high trust, high standards, no ego.

I also value the remote-first, flexible culture. I'm most productive when I can do deep work on my own schedule while staying tightly connected to my team through async communication and deliberate synchronous touchpoints. Pomelo's model fits that working style well.

## Why Now

Pomelo recently closed a $92M round and acquired the Doula Network, expanding into hybrid maternity care. The growth data science function has real leverage right now — the company is scaling fast, the patient population is growing, and the decisions made in the next 12–18 months about how to acquire and retain patients will shape the trajectory of the business. I want to be part of building that foundation, not inheriting it.

I'm excited about the scale of the problem, the strength of the team, and the opportunity to do work that genuinely matters. I'd love to be part of what Pomelo is building.
